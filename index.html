ANATOMY OF A DIAMOND

One of the most deﬁning characteristic of a diamond is its cut. A diamond's cut is an integral factor in determining its brilliance and ﬁre. Even a ﬂawless diamond will appear dull and muted if the cut is poor.

While high grades color, clarity, and carat weight also contribute to a diamonds appeal, it's the cut that determines the symmetry of the stones facets, its overall proportions, and its ability to reﬂect light.

HANDLE YOUR DIAMOND SPARINGLY

Diamonds are natural magnets for grease. When a diamond is handled, the oils from your ﬁngers adhere to the diamond’s surface and affect its brilliance and ﬁre.

CLEAN YOUR DIAMOND REGULARLY

A simple plan to keep your diamond jewelry looking beautiful is to soak it in a gentle degreasing solution, such as water with a few drops of mild dish soap. After you remove the diamond from the cleaning solution, use a soft, clean toothbrush to remove any remaining dirt. Use it to clean hard-to-reach places like the back of the diamond, which tends to collect the most oil and dirt.

Buying a diamond is far from being safe

You already know the 4c's - cut, color, clarity and carat, but dive deeper into the diamond buying process and you will find another C: certification. This may be the most important C because it ensures the other C's are in fact what they are said to be.

Protect your assets by following these simple steps

1.) Cross check your diamond and certificate. The inscription is on your diamond's girdle, ask your jeweler to guide you to make sure the numbers & inclusions match.

2.) GIA has a 'GIA Grading' number on each report. Kindly visit their website www.gia.edu and input your report/grading number to verify your certificate is authentic.
